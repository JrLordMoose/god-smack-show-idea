# GOD SMACK! — Image Generation Prompts

**Target tool:** Google Gemini (Imagen / Nano Banana)
**Visual DNA:** Berserk ink-grain · Devilman Crybaby bold color · Fullmetal Alchemist composition · Devil May Cry posing
**Palette to respect across all images:** deep bone / parchment cream, blood-red accents, muted gold leaf, warm near-black shadows. Avoid neon, avoid pastel, avoid cute anime.

A short global style stem you can paste before any prompt below for consistency:

> **Style stem:** "Original anime concept art in the spirit of Berserk and Devilman Crybaby — heavy hand-inked line work, oil-paint shadows, grainy parchment paper texture, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, blood-red and muted gold accents on a bone-white / deep black palette, no neon, no pastel, no soft cute anime, cinematic 35mm grain, painted by Kentaro Miura / Yoh Yoshinari / Hiroyuki Imaishi, single key visual, high contrast."

## Slide 04 (Tone Reel) — Contact-Sheet Prompt

You have two ways to generate the 8 tone frames: as a single 8-up contact sheet (one image), or individually (eight images). Individual is safer for final fidelity. The contact sheet is faster and ensures color/lighting consistency across all 8 frames in one pass.

### Option A — Single 8-up Contact Sheet (recommended for first pass)

> **Prompt:** "Generate a single illustrated **tone reel contact sheet** for an original anime pitch titled *God Smack!*. The image is laid out as a **2-row × 4-column grid** of 8 separate scene panels on a bone-white parchment background, divided by thin 4px bone-cream gutter rules. **Every panel must share the same color treatment, paper grain, and ink-line weight** — they read as frames pulled from the same animated production. No captions, no labels, no text inside any panel. Each panel is 3:4 vertical aspect ratio inside the grid. Style: hand-inked line work in the spirit of Kentaro Miura (Berserk) and Yoh Yoshinari, oil-paint shadows, grainy parchment paper texture, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, blood-red and muted gold accents on a bone-white and deep ink palette. No neon, no pastel, no cute anime, no chibi, no 3D-render or glossy CGI, no watermark.
>
> The 8 panels, reading left-to-right top-to-bottom:
>
> 1. **RITUAL** — A torchlit temple interior. A masked initiate kneels at a low stone altar carrying a small offering of cowrie shells and gold leaf. Red light beams from above through smoke. Sacred and threatening.
> 2. **VIOLENCE** — A single anime impact frame: a god mid-strike, body torquing into an open-palm hit that sends a concentric shockwave of blood-red ink across the frame. Speed lines made of brush ink. Blood arc curving across the composition like calligraphy.
> 3. **SCALE** — A tiny lone human figure standing on a small balcony before a colossal mile-tall stepped pyramid of obsidian and gold filling the panel. Cosmic indifference. Painted matte-painting style.
> 4. **INTIMACY** — Tight close-up of two faces almost touching in low light. One face is frightened, eyes wide, breath visible in cold air. The other has a faint divine glow under the skin, unreadable. Single oil-lamp light source.
> 5. **HORROR** — A defeated god is being erased: a vertical fissure of pure white-gold light splits down their chest, light bleeding out into the dark space around them. Their face is calm — they know they're being un-made. Beautifully horrifying.
> 6. **MOTION** — A Stridemark-class god mid-blink: their body is duplicated as a chain of after-images streaking diagonally across the panel, leaving golden after-trails. One foot is still in this world, the other is gone. Ink dragged across paper.
> 7. **MYTH** — A close-up of an ancient sacred mural carved into bone-colored stone, depicting an Ordained god being worshipped by tiny human figures. Partially weathered, woodblock-print line work, gold leaf embedded in the carving. Composition graphic and centered, like a page from an illuminated manuscript.
> 8. **DECAY** — A ruined monument to a Faded god in a wasteland of ash. The god's stone statue is shattered, face replaced by an empty oval of nothing. Wind carries gray ash particles diagonally. A single white prayer-bead sits on the broken altar at its feet. Cold, mournful, absolute silence.
>
> Final image aspect ratio: **16:9 landscape**. Resolution: as high as the model supports. All 8 panels equally lit, equally weighted, no panel more saturated or sharper than another."

### Option B — Eight Separate Generations

If the contact sheet drifts in style across panels, fall back to generating each frame individually using the prompts already listed under **Slide 04 — Tone Reel** above (04-01 through 04-08). For consistency, always paste the global **style stem** at the top of the file before each prompt, and keep the **same seed** across all 8 generations if your tool supports it.

### Consistency tips

- **Always include the style stem** before any tone-reel prompt — it locks the palette and ink treatment.
- **Use the negative prompt:** `no neon colors, no pastel, no chibi or moe styling, no cute anime, no Disney style, no 3D-render look, no glossy CGI, no captions or text in frame, no watermark, no extra fingers.`
- **If a frame comes back too colorful**, re-prompt with the suffix `"limited palette — only blood red, muted gold, bone white, and warm near-black."`
- **If line work feels too clean**, add `"rough brush ink, hatching, visible paper grain."`
- **If figures feel too detailed for a mood frame**, add `"silhouette-forward composition, character read second to atmosphere."`
- After generation, run a separate "upscale and clean line work, preserve original grain" pass if your tool supports it.
